Facing the future

lettera ai Capi di Stato

To the kind Staff of the President
of the United States of America,
Mr. Barack Obama,


I send you my most distinguished regards.

Very courteously, conceive me some words in order to introduce a delicate situation and a propositive synthesys.

Today we all on this world are facing a fast and great change. Probably the present financial fall will end soon and our economies will begin again to grow. But the natural resources are not enough for the needs of seven billions people and we can suppose that the more we will try to grow the more we will experience economic problems. People of our Countries will have more and more serious problems and their Governments consequently too.

Also environmental issues and the exponential advancement of technology will increase consistency and velocity of a change that will become more and more difficul to be managed with our present social organizations. Our cultural, economic, political organizations are conceptually very old and of static type. On the contrary, in order to live well a rapid and wide change, we urgently need a comprehensive reorganization on dynamic basis.

We also urgently need organizations that, instead to divide people in castes and factions, always fighting among them weakening the society, could favour a general reunion in a unic compact harmonized social strenght that has superated the individual problems and could be powerfully focused on important collective aims. Probably, the more united we will become the more we will be able to successfully superate this chaotic phase of the human history.

Lastly, the success obtained by a Country easily would be replicated by other Countries. And, being the comprehensive project described in the attached sheet a democratic flowering, if the other Countries, also those with very large and therefore threatening populations, will follow it too, also the serious danger of a final global conflict will consequently diminuish.

Kind Sirs, these times are very worrisome. Only for this reason I am daring to propose you the project for a “global progress in four points”. I hope you can forgive my impudence.


Danilo D'Antonio
