premised that:

today the roles of the public employment are assigned to persons chosen through special procedures that would select the fittest, among the many who would get them, and that, once chosen the persons estimated adapt, it is use to assign to them that job for their whole life;


considered that:

  • the available roles are quite inferior in number respect to that, not only of the aspirers, but, that is thing much more important, also of those who are widely fit and provided with the requested requirements;

  • the greater part of the fit persons are forgotten by the actual techniques of selection of the workers, that have the old-fashioned conviction to can determine a hypothetical and nonexistent winner there where is instead possible to determine a thriving rose of people capable and having the just requirements;

  • for the motives of the two previous points, what is assigned to those chosen few people, it is not mainly a work, but a true and exact privilege, particularly respect to the other people that are remained unjustly excluded and more in general respect to the rest of the society;

  • this privilege is completely unjustified, because, if we recognize the society, in its entirety, to have the right to make use of the public assets and services, in the same manner we should recognize to it, equally in its entirety, the right of fair participation to the administration, creation and offer of such assets and services;

  • a public role should not be of exclusive property of someone (like instead it happens with the actual allocation for life to an individual) precisely for its same origin and definition of "Public";

considered also that:

  • It is evident duty of a Republic to dissolve every obstacle that impedes equal opportunities to all the citizens and favour instead their concrete participation to the administrative organization of their Country, in a way that every person could be conducted to a full development;

  • actuating such participation of all the suitable workers to the cultural, economical, political and social organization of our Country, we would obtain various concrete benefits: an enhancement of the comprehensive functionality of the system, a more just distribution of the wealth, an obvious sense of realized justice and a spirit of instinctive and fraternal collaboration that would immediately spread inside the whole society;

  • allowing a free, effective and full participation of the Citizens to the activities of the State, this would not more separate from them, as it is today, with the iniquities, incomprehensions and conflicts that continually consequently rise and grieve us;

  • the Public Administration, once become democratic, would not more be only a simple occupation and source of income for the citizens, but would gain right dignity by be also school in which to learn and practice the ways of the civil life, fully realizing its potentialities of privileged medium of organization of the State;

besides, premised that

our economical order along the time has been strongly unbalanced towards the private sector, and its unusual weight and power, increased to the detriment of the public sector, are causing more and more serious problems, to which it is more and more difficult to give remedy;


considered that

such strong imbalance was born mainly for the active contribution of connivances and corruptions accumulated in the decades of permanence by the designated for life in the Public Administration, and for the the passive acceptation of the citizenship, that did not like any more a PA not corresponding to its necessities;


finally, considered that

the society use for very long time only the approach of knowledge and intervention of focused, sectorial, specialized kind, forgetting the existence of the complementary systemic, organic, holistic approach, that is instead necessary when we face complex systems like those biological and social;


the signed citizen


invites the Parliament

  1. to make real the appellative of "public" in the system of the Public Employment, by abolishing the iniquitous privilege of the role assigned for life to the person, in favor of a fair and wise distribution of the tasks among all those who want to get them and could show to possess the necessary requirements;

  2. to engage itself in a serious work in order to rebalance our economical system, by leading back many of the activities today private, first those of fundamental importance for our society, inside the public sector, till to reach half of the economical weight of the whole system;

  3. to realize the ideal of a social system inspired to the participation, therefore to a greater concord and functionality, guaranteeing a minimum job to every citizen of this Republic and by assisting him/her with a basic income in the short periods between an allocation and another;

  4. to carrying out the rotation of the workers through two different kinds of movement: by moving the people in a same and in a different field of skill. By moving them inside a same specialized branch, it is possible thecontribution of a larger number of people and it is prevented the corruption. By moving them in different fields, it is possible the sharing of the knowledges and a distributed progress, besides gaining a very useful organic perception of the whole. Both the movements favor the open-mindedness and the capacity to communicate, as well as the rise of a strong feeling for public interest.

Seen all that,


the undersigned declares finally that

so much he considers important this reform that, in the case it would be officially approved in our Country, a comprehensive sum of sixty hundredths of euro (or equivalent value) for every citizen will appear to him a good expense, to assign therefore to the Eudemony Laboratory of Social Reseaches that ideated it, in order to use this money to divulge the organic vision of the reality and the holistic approach.


Grateful for the attention, he thanks,
