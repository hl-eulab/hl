THE PERVERSE ROLE OF JOURNALISTS


Until a few decades ago, Humanity had begun to evolve and progress at a good pace, with positive results already emerging. Then, journalists multiplied, springing up everywhere in droves. They started pouring immense amounts of sterile, daily chronicles onto unprepared human beings—at first paralyzing them and now almost entirely suppressing our evolutionary process. If we look around today, we see nothing but a vain, repetitive everyday life. Journalists have, in fact, killed our bright future by perpetuating the filthy present, a reality incapable of change because it is firmly anchored to itself by journalists. It is therefore essential to remember that nothing holds absolute value.

Least of all does information have value when it never moves beyond a mere account of existing situations (which we already know in depth) and blocks the path to broad and courageous transformative visions. Foremost among these is the Democratic Transition—the periodic return to the People of those sacred, inalienable common properties: public jobs, powers, and incomes. And yet, this concept remains entirely absent from the flood of information that dominates us. Even though we are small in the face of such global phenomena, let us commit to understanding how things truly stand. Knowledge is already power, because everything always begins there. Information must no longer act as a screen preventing access to the broader knowledge that Humanity has always needed.

Danilo D'Antonio, ideas wholesaler
